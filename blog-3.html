1 BHK vs 2 BHK in Sobha One World Hoskote — Which Should You Buy in 2026?

Category: Buyer Guide  |  Date: May 2026  |  Reading Time: 6 min

The Configuration Landscape at Sobha One World Hoskote

Sobha The One World Hoskote — also called Sobha One World Hoskote and Sobha Codename Trinity — offers a comprehensive range of configurations. The core options are: 1 BHK (740 sq.ft, ₹1.09 Cr), 2 BHK Luxe (1,063 sq.ft, ₹1.58 Cr), 2 BHK Grande (1,204 sq.ft, ₹1.77 Cr), plus premium 3 BHK and 4 BHK options. For most first-time buyers and investors, the decision comes down to one question: 1 BHK or 2 BHK? That's exactly what this guide covers — price, yield, EMI, and which profile wins with which configuration.

The Case for 1 BHK — Maximum Yield, Minimum Outlay

If you're a pure investor? The 1 BHK wins on yield metrics. Full stop. Entry is low — ₹1.09 Crore, making it one of the few 1 BHK flats in Hoskote below ₹1.5 Crore with a Tier-1 developer name attached. EMI at 8.75% over 20 years: roughly ₹72,000/month. Expected rent post-possession: ₹20,000–₹28,000/month. That's 3.5–4.5% gross yield — genuinely competitive. And 1 BHKs rent faster. Lower vacancy periods, faster capital recovery. Single professionals and young couples working in Whitefield and ITPL are exactly the tenant profile this project will attract in 2031. Small unit. Big returns. Simple math.

The Case for 2 BHK — Flexibility, Family Use & Better Capital Appreciation

The 2 BHK wins on versatility and absolute appreciation. A 2 BHK can be your long-term family home, a generous rental flat, or both — sequentially. Expected rent: ₹30,000–₹42,000/month for the Grande. The 2 BHK Grande at 1,204 sq.ft feels genuinely spacious — Sobha's floor plan efficiency makes every square foot usable. Over the past decade, 2 BHKs in East Bangalore have appreciated faster in absolute rupee terms than 1 BHKs, simply because they cater to a much wider demographic at possession. EMI for the 2 BHK Grande: approximately ₹1.17 Lakhs/month at 8.75% over 20 years. If you're buying for family use with an eye on rental income later, the 2 BHK is the smarter pick in the long run.

Which BHK Is Right for Your Buyer Profile?

Your answer depends entirely on your profile. Pure investor after maximum rental yield with minimum capital outlay? The 1 BHK wins. Young couple planning to move in at possession? The 2 BHK Luxe is the right step. Established family needing room for parents or kids? The 2 BHK Grande — or consider upgrading to a 3 BHK. NRI looking for a reliable rental asset that doubles as a holiday home during India visits? The 2 BHK Grande is made for you. There's no wrong answer here — just the right one for your specific situation. That's what our advisors help you figure out, without any sales pressure.

The Floor Effect — How Floor Choice Changes the Equation

In a G+44 tower, the floor you pick actually changes the investment equation. Units above the 30th floor command floor-rise premiums — but they also attract higher resale values and premium rents because of the views, privacy, and air quality. For investors, a 1 BHK on a high floor can outperform a lower-floor 2 BHK on rental premium percentage. For end-users, floors 15 to 25 are typically the sweet spot — high enough to escape street-level noise, low enough to stay below the most expensive floor-rise bands. Worth thinking about before finalising your unit selection.

Final Recommendation

Here's the short version. Investor? Go 1 BHK, mid-to-high floor. Family buyer or balanced use/rental? Go 2 BHK Grande. Budget allows? The 3 BHK Grande at ₹2.22 Cr offers the single best long-term capital appreciation in absolute rupee terms across the entire Sobha One World configuration mix. Whatever you choose, timing matters most — register before May 20, 2026. Prices will rise by 5% at the official RERA launch. And that ₹5–8 Lakh difference is real money.
