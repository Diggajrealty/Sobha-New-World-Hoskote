Can NRIs Buy Property in India in 2026? — The Legal Foundation

Yes, absolutely. Under the guidelines laid out by FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act), Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) and Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs) can freely purchase residential and commercial property in India without requiring any special permission from the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). There is no limit on the number of properties you can acquire. While the rules strictly exclude agricultural land, farmhouses, and plantation property, premium residential apartments like those in Sobha One World Hoskote are fully permitted and encouraged. Which Account to Pay From — NRE, NRO, or FCNR?

When investing in Indian real estate, choosing the right banking channel is critical for future repatriation of funds.

NRE (Non-Resident External) account: This is a fully repatriable account. It can be used directly for property purchases, and both the principal amount and any interest earned are fully repatriable without tax deductions at the source in India.
NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) account: This account is primarily for managing Indian income, such as rental yields or salary if you previously worked in India. Funds here are only partially repatriable (up to ₹1 million per financial year) after paying applicable taxes.
FCNR (Foreign Currency Non-Resident) account: These are fixed deposits held in foreign currency, protecting you from exchange rate fluctuations. They can be broken to fund a property purchase and are fully repatriable.
For property acquisitions, we strongly recommend using an NRE account. An NRE account property purchase ensures maximum repatriation flexibility when you eventually sell the asset. Alternatively, the NRO account India property route is suitable if you plan to reinvest the funds within India. FCNR property investment is also highly viable for large ticket sizes.

Tax Implications for NRI Property Buyers

Understanding the tax landscape is vital. Stamp duty and registration charges are exactly the same as for resident Indians (approximately 5.6% in Karnataka). GST is applicable at 5% on under-construction properties. Regarding TDS, the buyer must deduct 1% TDS on property purchases above ₹50 Lakhs and deposit it with the IT department (our team assists with this compliance). Rental income is taxable in India at applicable slab rates. For capital gains on a future sale, Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG) tax is 20% with indexation benefits (applicable if sold after 2 years). NRIs can reinvest these gains into a new residential property in India under Section 54 to claim significant capital gains exemptions. While we provide guidance on NRI property tax India 2026, stamp duty NRI Bangalore, and LTCG NRI property India, we always strongly recommend consulting a qualified CA for personalized tax planning.
